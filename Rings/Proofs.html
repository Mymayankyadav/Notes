The result follows by induction applied to \(x'\) in place of \(x\) .

4.5.11 Gauss Lemma

If \(f(X)\) is primitive and irreducible in \(R[X]\). then \(f(X) =g(X)h(X)\) with \(g(X), h(X) \in K[X]\).We can write \(g(X) = (a/b)p(X)\) and \(h(X) =(c/d)q(X)\) with \(a, b, c, d \in R^*, p(X), q(X ) \in R[X]^*\) and both \(p(X)\) and \(q(X)\) primitive. Now substituting, we can write \(f(X) = (\alpha/\beta)p(X)q(X)\) for some \alpha , \(\beta \in R*\) with \(\alpha , \beta\) coprime. Thus we get \(\beta(X) = \alpha p(X )q(X)\) and hence comparing contents on either side, we have ,\(\beta c(f) = \alpha c(p)c(q)\) , i.e., \(\beta = \alpha\) which means \(f(X) = p(X)q(X)\) in \(R[X]\) contradicting the irreducibility of \(f(X)\).
